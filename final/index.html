Partnership

Making a commitment to work with a coach is an important investment in yourself. Through this partnership, we will work together and identify your needs, challenges and goals. The client and coach work collaboratively to achieve the desired outcomes you want for yourself. The client creates the agenda for each meeting, which typically occurs once a week.

Process

As we move through our sessions we will strategize and reflect on sought after goals. I will use powerful questioning to help you dig deeper and to create opportunities for you to challenge yourself. With the use of direct and effective communication we will establish a dialogue and discussion which will lead you to greater empowerment.

About

Kathleen is a mother of three teenage/young adult children who have all been impacted by ADD to varying degrees. She has invested extensive time and resources over the years researching ADD by conferring with other professionals, attending lectures on ADD and reading books on the subject. Her most recent education on ADD/ADHD came in the form of formal coach training. This was completed through an accredited ADD coaching school ADDCA - ADD Coach Academy. ADDCA is a coaching school recognized by the governing body ICF - International Coach Federation of which she is a member.

Kathleen’s undergraduate studies were completed at Towson State University where she received a BS in General Education. For the past three years she has coached adults as well as students from college age down to elementary school age. She has also hosted presentations on ADD/ADHD and has worked in the public school system working with elementary age children.

FAQ

How does the ADHD coaching relationship work?

Coaching starts with a phone conversation to assess the clients particular challenges and determine the clients needs and desired outcomes. The coach & client meet weekly, scheduling a mutually agreed upon time that takes place either in person or by phone. The client brings the agenda of what he or she would like to focus on for each appointment while the coach supports the client by customizing strategies that help ensure the client finds success.

How do I know if I’m ready for ADHD/ADD coaching?

If you are ready to make positive changes in your life and you are looking to experience growth, be it personal, professional, or relational than coaching is right for you. Clients need to possess a commitment to be open to new ideas, strategies and approaches. They also need to be willing to implement the needed action steps to create the positive growth they desire.

How long does the coaching relationship last?

The length of the coaching relationship varies depending on the scope of the client’s needs and goals. I ask all clients to consider a 3 month commitment in order establish a working relationship and to promote noticeable change and progress. Many clients notice, the longer they are committed to the coaching process, the more results they achieve.
